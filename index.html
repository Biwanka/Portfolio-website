Who I am

Traditional and Digital Artist

I have been creative since I was young, starting with dance. Over time, I discovered my passion for visual art, which led me to pursue art studies in college. I am an artist who thrives on exploring new ways of making art and experimenting with different media. Many of my works explore personal aspects of my life. I am deeply inspired by the people around me and by my environment. Memory and identity are recurring themes in my work.

I use my art as a form of expression, especially when words are difficult. Some of my work addresses my disabilities, ADHD, dyslexia, chronic migraines, nerve damage, and serves as a way to process, accept, and communicate these experiences. While not all of my work is about these topics, they influence the way I think, create, and interact with the world.
